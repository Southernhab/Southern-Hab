Industrial, municipal and utility properties need vegetation kept under control without interfering with access or daily operations.

Invasive-Plant Control

Invasive plants can spread beyond the original problem area and make normal maintenance more difficult. Early treatment can keep a manageable issue from becoming a larger project.

We begin by identifying the target species and determining how much of the site is affected. We look at plant density and growth stage. We also review access and nearby sensitive areas. Common concerns may include cogongrass, Chinese tallow, kudzu and privet.

Once the treatment area is defined, we complete the work using methods suited to the plant and the property. That may include cutting, mowing, mechanical treatment or herbicide application. Established infestations often need follow-up treatment. We can map affected areas and track progress over time.

What the client can expect: Target species identified and treated in the field, with mapped problem areas and a clear follow-up schedule when continued control is needed.

We can document the treatment limits and completed work with maps and photographs. Recurring service can also be arranged to keep the area accessible.

Our work may include burn-unit layout, firebreak preparation, timing, field implementation and post-burn review. We document completed work and monitor how the vegetation responds. Future burn plans can then be adjusted as the property and management goals change.

What the client can expect: Burn units reviewed and prepared in the field, followed by organized implementation, documentation and future management planning.

Nuisance-Wildlife Management
