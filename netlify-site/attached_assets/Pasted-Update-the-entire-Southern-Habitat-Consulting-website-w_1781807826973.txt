COLOR PALETTE

Create and use these global CSS variables:

--color-forest: #1F3A2E;
--color-pine: #13271F;
--color-cream: #F3EFE4;
--color-warm-white: #FAF9F5;
--color-charcoal: #252A27;
--color-moss: #66765A;
--color-gold: #B58A48;
--color-earth: #76543C;
--color-border: #D8D3C7;

Use the colors as follows:

- Warm white for main page backgrounds and long-form reading areas
- Warm cream for service cards, alternating sections, forms, and callout areas
- Forest green for primary buttons, major headings, important links, section accents, icons, and dividers
- Deep pine for the footer, mobile navigation, dark calls to action, and dark hero overlays
- Charcoal for body text, labels, supporting headings, and navigation text on light backgrounds
- Moss green only for small accents, borders, secondary icons, and eyebrow headings
- Muted gold only for small accents, active indicators, underlines, focus rings, and section dividers
- Earth brown only for restrained timber-related accents or small graphic details

Do not use:
- Pure black backgrounds
- Pure white throughout the entire site
- Bright gradients
- Neon greens
- Gold as a large background
- Moss green for normal body text
- Heavy shadows
- Glass effects
- Large rounded pill buttons
- Decorative textures
- Camouflage
- Wood-grain backgrounds

COLOR BALANCE

Keep the site light and readable.

Use approximately:
- 45% warm white
- 25% warm cream
- 15% forest green
- 8% deep pine
- 4% moss green
- 2% muted gold
- 1% earth brown

Do not make every section dark green.

SECTION BACKGROUNDS

Use this general pattern:

ACCESSIBILITY

Ensure all text meets WCAG AA contrast.

At minimum:
- Normal text contrast: 4.5:1
- Large text contrast: 3:1
- Buttons, links, and controls remain clearly visible
- Focus states are obvious
- Actions are not communicated by color alone

Approved combinations:
- Charcoal on warm white
- Charcoal on warm cream
- Forest green on warm white
- Forest green on warm cream
- Warm white on forest green
- Warm white on deep pine
- Cream on deep pine

Avoid:
- Moss body text on cream
- Gold body text on white
- Light gray text on cream
- Forest-green text on dark photography
- White text over bright images without an overlay

HERO OVERLAYS

Where text appears over images:
- Use deep pine #13271F
- Overlay opacity around 45% to 60%
- Keep headings and supporting text readable on desktop and mobile
- On mobile, use a stronger overlay or move text below the image when necessary
