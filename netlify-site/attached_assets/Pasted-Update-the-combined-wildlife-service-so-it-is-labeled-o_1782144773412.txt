Wildlife Management

Wildlife management starts with understanding how animals are using the property and what the habitat provides throughout the year.

We review food, cover, water, travel corridors and seasonal use. Harvest history, camera data and field observations may also help identify population trends and areas of heavy activity. The owner’s goals guide the plan, whether the property is managed for hunting, recreation, wildlife viewing or a combination of uses.

Field work may include habitat improvements, vegetation management, opening maintenance, access work and monitoring. Mapping, camera surveys and observation records can be used to track changes over time. The plan can then be adjusted as habitat conditions and wildlife use change.

Nuisance-Wildlife Management

Nuisance wildlife can affect access, timber, agricultural land, food plots and wildlife populations.

Beavers may flood roads, trails or timber stands. Feral hogs can damage food plots, fields and planted areas. Predators may place added pressure on turkey, deer and other wildlife when habitat conditions support high numbers.

We review the damage, the species involved and the conditions contributing to the problem. This may include water levels, food sources, cover, travel routes and access points. Removal may be part of the work, but it is not treated as the only solution. The broader plan may also include habitat changes, vegetation work, exclusion, monitoring and water-management improvements.

What the Client Can Expect

A field-based management plan supported by habitat assessment, wildlife observations, mapped activity areas and practical work designed to improve wildlife use while reducing damage and unwanted pressure on the property.
