Industrial Wildlife Management

Wildlife activity on industrial and municipal properties can create safety concerns, damage infrastructure and interfere with daily operations. Airports may need help reducing wildlife use near runways and flight paths. Beavers can block drainage systems and raise water levels around roads or facilities. Alligators may also create concerns around detention ponds and other areas used by employees or the public.

We begin by reviewing the site and identifying the species, activity patterns and conditions drawing wildlife to the property. That may include open water, food sources, vegetation, nesting areas and travel routes. We then develop a management plan that addresses both the immediate issue and the conditions that may cause it to return.

Work may include wildlife monitoring, habitat modification, vegetation management, exclusion, beaver control and alligator relocation where appropriate. Airport projects may also involve mapping wildlife activity and identifying areas where site conditions can be changed to reduce future use.

What the Client Can Expect

A site-specific wildlife-management plan supported by field inspections, activity mapping, practical habitat changes and direct response to wildlife issues affecting safety, drainage and site operations.
