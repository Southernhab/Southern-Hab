Feral swine activity is concentrated in Sections 1 (Bottomland) and 4 (Creek Corridor). Rooting damage is visible in moist soil areas adjacent to the creek. Activity appears to be a sounder of 6–10 animals based on camera evidence.

SHC recommends a targeted trapping effort in Sections 1 and 4 before fall 2026. A formal trapping proposal can be prepared upon request. Feral swine control in Alabama does not require a permit on private land.
